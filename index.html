About Me

Starting Fall 2025, I will join the O’Neill School of Public and Environmental Affairs (SPEA) at Indiana University Bloomington as an Assistant Professor.

I am currently a Postdoctoral Research Associate in the Department of Resource Economics at the University of Massachusetts Amherst, working with Prof. David Keiser.

My research focuses on the intersection of human and environmental systems, with special attention to health, land use, and water resources. My goals include:

  • Evaluating the economic and health impacts of land and water policies.
  • Developing Integrated Assessment Models (IAMs) to address water quality challenges, particularly nutrient pollution.

I earned a Ph.D. in Natural Resource Economics and Environmental Informatics from Virginia Tech (USA) and hold degrees in Development Economics (from Brazil) and Mechanical Engineering (from India).
